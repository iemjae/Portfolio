01 / What I Do

The Fine Print of Innovation

When companies launch products, there is an invisible layer underneath: standards, architectures, claims, protocols, firmware, and strategic intellectual property. My work is to analyze that layer.

Patent claims are not just legal text — they are precise technical descriptions of how innovations function. Reading them requires the same mind that designed them: an engineering mind.

I investigate how concepts from research become products, how products enter standards, and how standards generate intellectual property rights. Whether for enforcement, risk mitigation, or portfolio strategy — the goal is always the same: precise, defensible analysis that enables confident decisions.

My background in embedded systems, signal processing, and real-time firmware means I can trace a patent claim from language all the way to silicon.
